## StreetSafe Systems
# Security Incident Response Procedure  
---

### 1. Purpose  
StreetSafe is designed to help employees develop practical de-escalation skills for managing real-world, potentially volatile situations in public-facing environments. It focuses on pattern recognition and controlled response techniques to reduce risk, prevent escalation, and build confidence in handling difficult interactions.  

This document provides structured, scenario-based guidance to better prepare employees for situations they are likely to encounter in day-to-day operations.

---

### 2. Scope  
This procedure is intended for small businesses operating in public-facing environments where employees may encounter unpredictable or volatile behavior. It applies to all staff responsible for interacting with customers, clients, or members of the public during day-to-day operations.  

This guidance is used during situations involving conflict, agitation, or disruptive behavior, where de-escalation and controlled response are required to maintain safety.

---

### 3. Definitions  

**Mitigation**  
Actions taken to reduce the likelihood or impact of a potentially volatile or dangerous situation.

**Person of Interest (POI)**  
An individual whose behavior has been identified as requiring attention due to potential risk, disruption, or escalation.

**Threat Assessment**  
The process of evaluating a situation, individual, or environment to determine the level of risk and appropriate response.

**Pattern Recognition**  
The ability to identify recurring behaviors, environmental cues, or situational indicators that may signal an increased risk of escalation.

**Active Listening**  
A communication technique involving full attention, acknowledgment, and appropriate response to a subject in order to reduce tension and improve understanding.
